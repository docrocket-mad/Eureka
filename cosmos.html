🤯 Scale Facts
🚗
Driving to the Moon
If you could drive a car to the Moon at highway speed (100 km/h), it would take about 160 days non-stop. To the Sun: 170 years. To Neptune: 4,600 years.
💡
The Speed of Light Problem
Light travels at 300,000 km/second — fast enough to circle Earth 7 times in one second. Yet it takes 8 minutes to reach us from the Sun. From the nearest star: 4.2 years. When you look at stars, you're looking into the past.
🌍
Earth is a Pale Blue Dot
In 1990, Voyager 1 turned its camera back at Earth from 6 billion km away. Earth appeared as a fraction of a pixel. Carl Sagan called it "a mote of dust suspended in a sunbeam." Every human who ever lived, on that dot.
☀️
The Sun is Enormous
1.3 million Earths could fit inside the Sun. Yet the Sun is a medium-sized star. The largest known star, UY Scuti, is so big that if it replaced the Sun, it would engulf Jupiter. You are very, very small.
⏳ Cosmic Timeline
If the universe's 13.8 billion year history were compressed into one calendar year, all of recorded human history happens in the last 14 seconds of December 31st.
